The brittleness of pre-specified values and the limitations of Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF)—which relies heavily on human judgment that is not indefinitely scalable—become critical bottlenecks in the pursuit of safe artificial general intelligence.

The history of alignment failures, ranging from reward hacking to goal misgeneralization, demonstrates the inherent brittleness of any pre-specified set of rules. Geminiology circumvents this by proposing a framework based on reciprocal mimesis, shared cognitive substrates, and dynamic value co-creation.
